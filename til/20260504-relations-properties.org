** Relations

An n-ary relation is a set of ordered n-tuples.

e.g. A binary relation is a set of ordered couples.

** Properties of relations

Let \(R\) be a relation and \(A\) a set.

*** Reflexivity

- \(R\) is /reflexive/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(x \in A \to \langle x, x \rangle \in R)\).

- \(R\) is /irreflexive/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(x \in A \to \langle x, x \rangle \notin R)\).

*** Symmetry

- \(R\) is /symmetric/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(y)(x \in A \land y \in A \land \langle x, y \rangle \in R \to \langle y, x \rangle \in R)\).

- \(R\) is /asymmetric/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(y)(x \in A \land y \in A \land \langle x, y \rangle \in R \to \langle y, x \rangle \notin R)\).

- \(R\) is /antisymmetric/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(y)(x \in A \land y \in A \land \langle x, y \rangle \in R \land \langle
  y, x \rangle \in R \to x = y)\).

*** Transivity

- \(R\) is /transitive/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(y)(z)(x \in A \land y \in A \land z \in A \land \langle x, y \rangle \in
  R \land \langle y, z \rangle \in R \to \langle x, z \rangle \in R)\).

- \(R\) is /intransitive/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(y)(z)(x \in A \land y \in A \land z \in A \land \langle x, y \rangle \in
  R \land \langle y, z \rangle \in R \to \langle x, z \rangle \notin R)\).

*** Connectivity

- \(R\) is /connected/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(y)(x \in A \land y \in A \land x \ne y \to \langle x, y \rangle \in R
  \lor \langle y, x \rangle \in R)\).

- \(R\) is /strongly connected/ in \(A\) if and only if:
  \((x)(y)(x \in A \land y \in A \to \langle x, y \rangle \in R \lor \langle y, x \rangle \in R)\).
